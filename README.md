# HTSHouseRules
A brief collection of the house rules we use to play the game Here to Slay, in a remote page so I can share the rules using a QR code

## Draw Cards
- 1-2 Hero
- 3 Items (including cursed items)
- 4 Challenge
- 5 Magic
- 6 Modifiers

## On Your Turn
### 1 Action Point:
- Draw one card (roll 1d6 to determine which stack to draw from)
- Play a Hero, Item, or Magic card. You may roll for hero cards immediately upon playing them.
- Roll to use the effect of a hero card (once per turn unless a card states otherwise)

### 2 Action Points:
- Attack a Monster Card
- Draw 1 card from a stack of your choice
- Duel another player

### 3 Action Points:
- Discard every card in your hand (if you have any) and draw 5 cards

## Extra Rules
### Hand Size Limit
- Players must discard down to 7 cards if they have more than 7 cards in their hand before ending their turn.

### Win Condition Changes
- Games end when players have X amount of "Victory Points". (Where X is whatever the table decides it will be before the game starts)
- Each of the following following are ways to get Victory Points:
1. Having one hero of every class represented in your party counts for one single Victory Point. You lose this Victory Point if anything causes you to no longer have every class represented (Your party leader counts toward this total)
2. Each monster in your party counts toward one Victory Point Each
3. Each Banner in your party counts toward one Victory Point Each

### Party Leader Balancing
- The Fist of Reason: You may consume one single challenge card during a duel with another player to get +1 to your roll

### Duels
Dueling another player is a way to battle your opponents at the table directly. A duel must be initiated by the active player spending 2 Action Points on their turn and choosing a target opponent.

**General Rules**
- A player must have at least one hero in their party to initiate a duel
- If you cannot achieve any of the results of a duel, nothing happens after it is over
- Ties go to the defender
- Any abilities and modifiers may be used by any player to interfere with the duel

**Resolving Duels:**
1. Once a duel begins both players must first declare which hero from their party will be your champion and participate in the duel
2. After deciding which hero will be your champion, both players must roll 2d6 with the player who initiated the duel going first.
3. After both players have made their rolls, SUBTRACT the value of your respective Champion's ability from your roll, and the result will be the number that is used for the duel. (If a player rolls a 12 and their Champion has an ability that activates on +4, then the final result of the roll will be 12 - 4 = 8. There is no difference when using the Druid class, if the initial roll is 12 and their Druid activates on -4 it would still just be 12 - 4 = 8)
4. The winner of the duel is decided by whoever has the highest roll at the end of the modifications

**Duel Rewards**
- If the player initiates the duel loses, their Champion is **Destroyed** and must go to the discard pile
- If the player who initiates the duel wins, they may choose ONE from the following effects:
1. **STEAL** one hero from the Defender's Party
2. **DESTROY** one hero from the Defender's Party
3. Force the defender to **DISCARD** 3 cards from their hand
4. **STEAL** A Monster card from the Defender's Party
